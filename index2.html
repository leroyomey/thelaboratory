1. Introduction

Welcome to ReadLater! Your privacy is important to us. This Privacy Policy outlines how we collect, use, and protect your information when you use the ReadLater browser extension (the “Service”).

By using ReadLater, you agree to the terms outlined in this Privacy Policy.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Saved Articles
When you save an article using the ReadLater extension, the URL and title of the article are stored locally in your browser’s storage. We do not collect or have access to any saved articles, URLs, or any data related to your browsing history.

2.2 Chrome Storage Data
All saved articles (including URLs and titles) are stored using Chrome’s chrome.storage.sync feature, which enables the data to be synchronized across your devices that are signed into the same Chrome account.

2.3 Non-Personal Data
We may collect anonymous, non-personal data such as aggregate usage statistics to help us improve the extension. This data does not identify individual users and may include:

  • Extension usage metrics (e.g., feature usage frequency)
  • Anonymous crash reports

3. How We Use Your Information

3.1 Stored Data
The URLs, titles, and reordering of saved articles are solely stored locally and synchronized using Chrome's storage system. This data is not transmitted to any external servers and is only used to display and manage your saved articles within the ReadLater extension.

3.2 Functional Data
Any changes you make to saved articles (editing titles, reordering, or deleting items) will update the stored data in your browser. These changes are reflected locally and across other Chrome browsers where you are logged in.

3.3 Improving the Service
We may use anonymous data to:

  • Understand how users interact with the extension
  • Identify issues, bugs, or crashes to improve performance and functionality
  • Introduce new features or improve existing features
